# Zebra Puzzle Reasoning Prompt

You are tasked with solving the famous Zebra Puzzle (also known as Einstein's Riddle). This is a classic logic puzzle that requires systematic reasoning to deduce the solution.

## The Puzzle (Life International, 1962)

There are five houses. Each has a different color, each houses a person of a different nationality, each person has a different favorite drink, smokes a different brand of cigarettes, and keeps a different pet. No owners have the same pet, smoke the same brand of cigarette, or drink the same beverage.

## The Clues

1. The Englishman lives in the red house.
2. The Spaniard owns the dog.
3. Coffee is drunk in the green house.
4. The Ukrainian drinks tea.
5. The green house is immediately to the right of the ivory house.
6. The Old Gold smoker owns snails.
7. Kools are smoked in the yellow house.
8. Milk is drunk in the middle house.
9. The Norwegian lives in the first house.
10. The man who smokes Chesterfields lives in the house next to the man with the fox.
11. Kools are smoked in the house next to the house where the horse is kept.
12. The Lucky Strike smoker drinks orange juice.
13. The Japanese smokes Parliaments.
14. The Norwegian lives next to the blue house.

## Your Task

Using deductive reasoning, work through the clues systematically to determine:

**Now, who drinks water? Who owns the zebra?**

## Instructions for Reasoning

1. Start by creating a grid or table to track the attributes: House position (1-5), Color, Nationality, Drink, Cigarette, and Pet.
2. Apply each clue one by one, marking what you know for certain and what possibilities remain.
3. Use the process of elimination to narrow down options.
4. Pay attention to positional clues (left/right, next to, immediately to the right).
5. Once you have placed all attributes, identify who drinks water and who owns the zebra.

## Response Format

Provide your answer in the following format:

```
Solution:
- The [Nationality] drinks water.
- The [Nationality] owns the zebra.
```
